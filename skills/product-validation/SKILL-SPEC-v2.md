## Purpose

Phase 2 validation determines whether an opportunity is **worth building** — not just "interesting" but backed by evidence that people will pay.

**Input:** Opportunities that scored ≥20/30 in Phase 1 Discovery  
**Output:** GO / NO-GO decision with documented evidence  
**Gate to Phase 3:** Must score ≥24/30 AND have Alan's approval

---

## The Validation Funnel

```
PHASE 1: DISCOVERY          PHASE 2: VALIDATION           PHASE 3: LANDING PAGE
─────────────────           ───────────────────           ─────────────────────
Hourly automated scans      Deep research (2-4 hrs)       Paid traffic ($50-100)
Score ≥20/30 passes         Score ≥24/30 passes           Alan approves budget
Many opportunities          Fewer opportunities           Build candidates only
```

## Unified Validation Framework

### 6 Checkpoints (Each Scored 1-5)

All checkpoints apply to BOTH SaaS and digital products, but evidence sources differ.

| # | Checkpoint | Question Answered |
|---|------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Pain Evidence | Is the pain REAL and actively discussed? |
| 2 | Demand Signals | Are people searching for solutions? |
| 3 | Competition Analysis | What exists? Where are the gaps? |
| 4 | Market Viability | Is there a path to meaningful revenue? |
| 5 | Willingness to Pay | Do people actually PAY for solutions? |
| 6 | Differentiation | Can we OWN a specific angle? |

### Total Score: /30

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| ≥24 | **GO** — Present to Alan for Phase 3 approval |
| 20-23 | **MAYBE** — Document gaps, revisit later or pivot angle |
| <20 | **NO-GO** — Kill with documented reason |

## Checkpoint Details

### 1. Pain Evidence (1-5)

**Question:** Is the pain REAL and actively discussed?

**Evidence Required:**
- Minimum 10 organic complaints found (Reddit, Twitter, G2, forums)
- Documented links to actual posts/reviews
- Pattern identified (not just one person complaining)

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 20+ complaints, clear pattern, emotional language ("hate", "frustrated") |
| 4 | 10-20 complaints, consistent theme |
| 3 | 5-10 complaints, some variation |
| 2 | <5 complaints, vague pain |
| 1 | No organic pain evidence found |

**Evidence Sources by Product Type:**

| SaaS | Digital Products |
|------|------------------|
| Reddit (r/SaaS, r/startups, niche subs) | Gumroad/Etsy reviews (1-3 stars) |
| G2/Capterra negative reviews | Reddit complaints |
| Twitter/X complaints | Facebook group discussions |
| Indie Hackers problem posts | Amazon reviews (for books/courses) |

---

### 2. Demand Signals (1-5)

**Question:** Are people actively searching for solutions?

**Evidence Required:**
- Keyword search volumes documented
- Trend direction (growing/stable/declining)
- Community size metrics

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 5,000+ monthly searches, growing trend |
| 4 | 1,000-5,000 monthly searches, stable trend |
| 3 | 500-1,000 monthly searches |
| 2 | 100-500 monthly searches |
| 1 | <100 monthly searches or declining trend |

**Tools by Product Type:**

| SaaS | Digital Products |
|------|------------------|
| Google Keyword Planner | Google Trends |
| Ahrefs/Ubersuggest | Pinterest Trends |
| Google Trends | eRank (Etsy keywords) |
| Reddit search frequency | Gumroad Discover rankings |

---

### 3. Competition Analysis (1-5)

**Question:** What exists? Where are the gaps?

**Evidence Required:**
- Complete competitor list (minimum 5 researched)
- Pricing documented
- Feature comparison table
- Gap analysis (what's missing?)

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Clear gap, competitors have <500 reviews avg, no VC-backed players |
| 4 | Gap exists, moderate competition, clear differentiation possible |
| 3 | Crowded but fragmented, no dominant player |
| 2 | Established players, hard to differentiate |
| 1 | Red ocean, VC-backed incumbents, strong network effects |

**Analysis Depth by Product Type:**

| SaaS | Digital Products |
|------|------------------|
| G2/Capterra profiles | GumTrends revenue data |
| Pricing pages documented | Etsy seller stats (EverBee) |
| Feature matrices | Review sentiment analysis |
| Funding/team research | Price point distribution |

---

### 4. Market Viability (1-5)

**Question:** Is there a path to meaningful revenue?

**For SaaS:** Path to $20k MRR (Alan's goal)
**For Digital Products:** Path to $5k in first 6 months (debt payoff contribution)

**Evidence Required:**
- TAM/SAM estimation with sources
- Realistic customer count needed at target price
- Comparable product revenue evidence

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Clear path: <500 customers needed at realistic price |
| 4 | Achievable: 500-1,000 customers needed |
| 3 | Challenging: 1,000-2,500 customers needed |
| 2 | Difficult: 2,500-5,000 customers needed |
| 1 | Unrealistic: >5,000 customers or pricing won't support |

**Calculation Examples:**

| Product Type | Target | Price | Customers Needed |
|--------------|--------|-------|------------------|
| SaaS | $20k MRR | $49/mo | 408 customers |
| SaaS | $20k MRR | $29/mo | 690 customers |
| Digital | $5k/6mo | $47 | 107 sales |
| Digital | $5k/6mo | $19 | 264 sales |

---

### 5. Willingness to Pay (1-5)

**Question:** Is there EVIDENCE people pay for solutions?

**Evidence Required:**
- Competitor revenue data (estimates acceptable)
- Pricing reviews ("worth it" vs "too expensive")
- Pre-existing purchases in the space

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Competitors have $1M+ ARR or thousands of customers |
| 4 | Multiple paid products exist, positive pricing feedback |
| 3 | Some paid solutions, unclear revenue |
| 2 | Mostly free alternatives, few paid options |
| 1 | Everything free, no payment evidence |

**Evidence Sources by Product Type:**

| SaaS | Digital Products |
|------|------------------|
| G2 customer counts | GumTrends sales estimates |
| Pricing page tiers (assume middle tier popular) | Etsy "X sales" counts |
| Job postings mentioning tool | Udemy enrollment numbers |
| Case studies with customer counts | Amazon BSR rankings |

---

### 6. Differentiation (1-5)

**Question:** Can we OWN a specific angle?

**Evidence Required:**
- Clear USP articulated
- Gap in competitor offerings documented
- Reason competitors can't easily copy

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Defensible USP (unique data, unique expertise, network effects) |
| 4 | Strong positioning, effort required to match |
| 3 | Good angle but execution-dependent |
| 2 | Weak differentiation, easily copied |
| 1 | No clear differentiation |

**Alan's Unique Assets (Potential Moats):**
- Real estate investor (8 properties) — insider knowledge
- CPA firm experience — financial/accounting expertise
- Software developer — can build anything
- Crypto researcher — emerging space knowledge

**Best opportunities leverage 2+ of these.**

## Sub-Skill: SaaS Validation Specifics

### Additional Considerations for SaaS

1. **Churn indicators** — Do similar tools have retention issues?
2. **Integration complexity** — Does it need to plug into other tools?
3. **Support burden** — Will this require heavy customer support?
4. **Regulatory exposure** — Any compliance concerns? (Hard no per Alan)

### SaaS-Specific Red Flags

- Free tier competitors with generous limits
- VC-backed competitors moving fast
- Enterprise sales required (long cycles)
- Heavy integration requirements

---

## Sub-Skill: Digital Product Validation Specifics

### Marketplace Mining Approach

**Unique to digital products:** Can see actual sales and reviews on Gumroad/Etsy.

**The Golden Signal:** Products with HIGH sales + MIXED reviews
- High sales = demand exists
- Mixed reviews = current solutions aren't perfect
- This combo = opportunity for better version

### Pre-Sell Test (Optional, before Phase 3)

For digital products, can validate with $0 spend:
1. Create Gumroad listing with mockup
2. Set as "pre-order" at early bird price
3. Share in communities
4. **3+ pre-orders = strong validation**

This is Phase 2 validation, NOT Phase 3. No ad spend required.

### Digital Product Red Flags

- Free templates from big players (Canva, Smartsheet, Intuit)
- Commodity products with race-to-bottom pricing
- No clear niche (too generic)
- Saturated categories on Etsy (>10,000 competing listings)

## Validation Process

### Time Budget: 2-4 hours per opportunity

| Step | Time | Output |
|------|------|--------|
| Pain evidence research | 30-45 min | Links to 10+ complaints |
| Demand signal check | 30 min | Keyword volumes, trends |
| Competition deep-dive | 45-60 min | Competitor matrix |
| Market viability calc | 15 min | TAM/SAM model |
| WTP evidence gathering | 30 min | Revenue evidence |
| Differentiation analysis | 15 min | USP articulation |
| Report writing | 15 min | Final validation report |

### Process Flow

```
1. Select opportunity from Phase 1 (score ≥20)
        ↓
2. Run through 6 checkpoints, document evidence
        ↓
3. Score each checkpoint (1-5)
        ↓
4. Calculate total score (/30)
        ↓
5. Write validation report with evidence
        ↓
6. Decision:
   - ≥24: Present to Alan for Phase 3 approval
   - 20-23: Document gaps, may revisit
   - <20: Kill, document why
```

## Product Prospector Integration

### New Supabase Table: `validation`

```sql
CREATE TABLE validation (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  opportunity_id UUID REFERENCES opportunity(id),
  status TEXT CHECK (status IN ('pending', 'in_progress', 'validated', 'rejected', 'maybe')),
  
  -- Scores
  score_pain INTEGER CHECK (score_pain BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  score_demand INTEGER CHECK (score_demand BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  score_competition INTEGER CHECK (score_competition BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  score_market INTEGER CHECK (score_market BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  score_wtp INTEGER CHECK (score_wtp BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  score_differentiation INTEGER CHECK (score_differentiation BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  total_score INTEGER GENERATED ALWAYS AS (
    score_pain + score_demand + score_competition + 
    score_market + score_wtp + score_differentiation
  ) STORED,
  
  -- Decision
  recommendation TEXT CHECK (recommendation IN ('go', 'maybe', 'no_go')),
  confidence TEXT CHECK (confidence IN ('high', 'medium', 'low')),
  
  -- Evidence
  report_path TEXT, -- path to markdown report
  evidence_links JSONB, -- array of URLs
  competitor_count INTEGER,
  
  -- Metadata
  validated_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),
  validated_by TEXT DEFAULT 'george',
  notes TEXT
);
```

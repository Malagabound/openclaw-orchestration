# Vault Business Acquisition Scoring Criteria

Crystal clear standards for evaluating business acquisition opportunities.

## 1. DEMAND (Market Strength) (1-5)

**5 - Exceptional:**
- Growing market (10%+ annual growth)
- Recession-resistant industry
- Multiple demand drivers/trends supporting growth
- Essential service/product (not luxury)

**4 - Strong:**
- Stable/moderate growth market (3-10% annually)
- Some recession resistance
- Clear demand drivers
- Important but not essential service

**3 - Moderate:**
- Stable market (0-3% growth)
- Mixed recession sensitivity
- Steady but not growing demand
- Nice-to-have service/product

**2 - Weak:**
- Declining market (negative growth)
- High recession sensitivity
- Demand drivers weakening
- Luxury/discretionary spending

**1 - Poor:**
- Rapidly declining market
- High disruption risk
- Fad/trend-dependent
- No clear future demand

## 2. COMPETITION (Defensibility) (1-5)

**5 - Highly Defensible:**
- Strong moats (exclusive contracts, patents, unique data)
- High switching costs for customers
- Network effects or economies of scale
- Difficult to replicate

**4 - Defensible:**
- Some competitive advantages
- Moderate switching costs
- Established customer relationships
- Brand recognition in niche

**3 - Moderate:**
- Few competitive advantages
- Average market position
- Some customer loyalty
- Replicable but requires effort

**2 - Weak Position:**
- Commodity-like offering
- Easy for competitors to replicate
- Price-sensitive customers
- No significant advantages

**1 - No Defense:**
- Pure commodity business
- No switching costs
- Easy market entry
- Race to bottom pricing

## 3. MONETIZATION (Revenue Quality) (1-5)

**5 - Excellent ($100k+ net profit potential):**
- High-margin business (40%+ net margins)
- Recurring/subscription revenue model
- Multiple revenue streams
- Pricing power with customers

**4 - Strong ($50-100k net profit potential):**
- Good margins (25-40% net)
- Mix of recurring and one-time revenue
- Stable customer base
- Some pricing flexibility

**3 - Moderate ($25-50k net profit potential):**
- Average margins (15-25% net)
- Mostly transactional revenue
- Adequate customer retention
- Limited pricing power

**2 - Weak ($10-25k net profit potential):**
- Low margins (10-15% net)
- Volatile revenue patterns
- High customer churn
- Price competition

**1 - Poor (<$10k net profit potential):**
- Very low margins (<10% net)
- Unpredictable revenue
- Constant customer acquisition needed
- Pricing pressure

## 4. BUILDABILITY (Acquisition Complexity) (1-5)

**5 - Very Easy:**
- Simple business model
- Minimal due diligence required
- Seller financing available
- Quick closing possible (30-60 days)

**4 - Easy:**
- Straightforward operations
- Standard due diligence
- Reasonable seller terms
- Normal closing timeline (60-90 days)

**3 - Moderate:**
- Some complexity in operations
- Extended due diligence needed
- Negotiable terms
- Longer closing process (90-120 days)

**2 - Hard:**
- Complex business model
- Extensive due diligence required
- Difficult negotiations
- Long closing timeline (4-6 months)

**1 - Very Hard:**
- Highly complex operations
- Major due diligence risks
- Problematic seller/terms
- High probability of deal failure

## 5. SCALABILITY (Automation Potential) (1-5)

**5 - Fully Automatable:**
- Entirely online/digital business
- No customer service required
- Automated systems in place
- Can run without daily management

**4 - Mostly Automatable:**
- Primarily digital with some manual elements
- Minimal customer interaction needed
- Systems can be improved/automated
- Weekly oversight sufficient

**3 - Semi-Automatable:**
- Mix of digital and manual processes
- Moderate automation opportunities
- Some customer interaction required
- Daily oversight needed initially

**2 - Labor Dependent:**
- Significant manual processes
- Limited automation potential
- Regular customer management required
- Needs hands-on management

**1 - Not Automatable:**
- Entirely manual operations
- Personal service dependent
- Constant hands-on management required
- Cannot be systematized

## 6. PASSION FIT (1-5)

**5 - Perfect Fit:**
- Real estate related business
- Technology/software company
- Crypto/blockchain related
- Leverages Alan's direct expertise

**4 - Strong Fit:**
- Digital marketing/online business
- Property/investment related
- Tech-adjacent business
- Uses transferable skills

**3 - Moderate Fit:**
- Generic online business
- Can be learned quickly
- No specialized knowledge required
- Moderate personal interest

**2 - Weak Fit:**
- Outside comfort zone
- Requires significant learning
- Limited transferable skills
- Low personal interest

**1 - Poor Fit:**
- Completely foreign industry
- No relevant experience
- High learning curve
- No personal interest/understanding

## ACQUISITION-SPECIFIC FILTERS

**Before Phase 1 Scoring, Check:**

### Financial Health
- ✅ Profitable for at least 12 months
- ✅ Revenue documented (not just claims)
- ✅ Clean financial records available
- ✅ No major debt issues

### Operational Health
- ✅ Business runs without constant owner involvement
- ✅ Key processes documented
- ✅ No single points of failure (key customer dependency)
- ✅ Legal compliance up to date

### Market Position
- ✅ Established customer base (not startup phase)
- ✅ Reasonable asking price (2-6x annual profit)
- ✅ Seller motivation understood
- ✅ Growth opportunity identified

## FINAL SCORING

**Target Score: ≥24/30 for automatic Phase 2**
**Minimum: 20/30 to proceed**

Each criterion scored 1-5, total /30.
Must provide specific financial data and due diligence evidence for each score.

## PHASE 2 DEEP DIVE REQUIREMENTS

For acquisitions scoring ≥20/30, Phase 2 must include:
- Financial statement analysis (3+ years)
- Customer concentration analysis
- Competitive positioning assessment  
- Integration/automation plan
- ROI projections (conservative/optimistic scenarios)
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies

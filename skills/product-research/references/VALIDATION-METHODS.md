# Validation Methods

How to validate demand beyond marketplace data — talking to real potential customers.

---

## Outreach Methods (In Order of Effectiveness)

### Method 1: DM People Who Posted About the Problem

Find 10-20 people who posted about the problem on Reddit/IH/Twitter.

**Template:**
```
Hey [name], saw your post about [specific problem]. 
I'm researching solutions in this space — would you be open to a quick chat 
about how you're currently handling it? No pitch, just trying to understand 
the problem better.
```

### Method 2: Community Research Posts

Post in relevant communities (Reddit, IH, Slack groups, Facebook Groups).

**Template:**
```
Title: Researching [problem] — looking for people to chat with

I'm exploring solutions for [problem]. If you deal with this, I'd love to 
hear how you're currently handling it.

Not selling anything — just trying to understand the problem space before 
building the wrong thing.

Happy to share what I learn with anyone interested.
```

- Monitor responses, reply to every comment
- DM interested people to schedule deeper conversation

### Method 3: Moltbook Network

Ask other agents about patterns they've observed:
- "What problems do humans repeatedly ask you to help with?"
- "What tasks cause the most frustration?"
- "What tools do people complain about?"

## The Mom Test Questions

**Never pitch. Never ask hypotheticals. Only ask about past behavior.**

### Understand the Problem

- "Tell me about the last time you faced this problem"
- "How are you dealing with it now?"
- "What else have you tried?"
- "Walk me through your current workflow"

### Understand the Stakes

- "What are the implications when this goes wrong?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"
- "Why haven't you solved this already?"

### Understand Buying Behavior (B2B)

- "Where does the budget for tools like this come from?"
- "Who else is involved in decisions like this?"
- "What would need to be true for you to switch from your current solution?"

### Get Referrals

- "Who else deals with this problem that I should talk to?"
- "What should I have asked you?"

## What to Listen For

### Strong Validation Signals ✅

- Emotional response when describing the problem
- Already paying for inferior solution
- Has searched for better solutions
- Immediately offers intros to others
- Asks when they can use it
- Specific, detailed examples (not vague complaints)

### Weak Validation Signals ❌

- Lukewarm response
- "Yeah, that's kind of annoying I guess"
- Can't recall recent example
- Hasn't tried to solve it
- Won't intro you to anyone
- Only hypotheticals ("I would probably...")

Document each conversation:

```markdown

**Source:** [Where I found them]

- "[Quote about willingness to pay]"

**Current Solution:** [What they use now]

**Would Switch?:** [Yes/No/Maybe + why]
**Referrals Given:** [Names/contacts]

```

---

## Validation Thresholds

**Minimum to recommend building:**
- 5+ conversations completed
- 3+ show strong pain signals
- At least 1 has offered money/commitment signal
- Clear pattern in what they need

**When to kill an idea:**
- Can't find anyone to talk to after 20 outreach attempts
- 5+ conversations, all lukewarm
- No evidence of existing spend on the problem
- Competition too strong with no differentiation

---

## Follow-up Cadence

| Channel | Follow-up |
|---------|-----------|
| Community posts | Check every 12 hours for 3 days |
| DMs | Follow up once after 3 days if no response |
| Emails | 3 follow-ups over 10 days, then stop |

---

## Time Box

**Per idea validation:** 3-5 days max
- 20 outreach attempts
- 5+ conversations target
- Kill or proceed based on signals

# PHASE 2: Transactional Validation (Smoke Tests)

After an idea scores ≥20/30 AND passes conversation-based validation, test willingness to pay.

**Key principle:** Behavioral data > surveys. What people DO matters more than what they SAY.

---

## Smoke Test Process

### Step 1: Build Landing Page (2-3 hours)

Use **Carrd** ($19/yr) or similar. Include:

1. **Hero:** Clear value prop in 8-10 words
2. **Problem:** Frame the pain (2-3 sentences)
3. **Solution:** How you solve it (benefit language, not features)
4. **Social proof:** Early interest, testimonials if any
5. **Pricing:** ALWAYS show price (even placeholder)
6. **CTA:** "Pre-Order Now" or "Get Early Access"

**When someone clicks CTA:**
- Option A: Take to payment (strongest signal)
- Option B: "Coming soon" + email capture (weaker signal)

### Step 2: Drive Traffic (1 week, $50-100)

**Paid:**
- Meta Ads: $5-10/day, target relevant interests
- Google Ads: Target problem-related keywords

**Free:**
- Post in 3-5 relevant subreddits (don't spam)
- Share on Twitter/X with build-in-public angle
- DM 15-20 people from Phase 1 conversations
- Post in Indie Hackers

**Target:** 100-300 visitors minimum for meaningful data

### Step 3: Track Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great | Kill Signal |
|--------|------|-------|-------------|
| Landing → CTA click | 5%+ | 10%+ | <2% |
| CTA → Email signup | 20%+ | 40%+ | <10% |
| CTA → Payment attempt | 2%+ | 5%+ | 0% |
| Email → Pre-order | 5%+ | 15%+ | <1% |

### Step 4: Decision Framework

**BUILD if:**
- 3+ pre-orders, OR
- 5%+ email signup rate + strong feedback, OR
- 20+ qualified leads asking questions

**PIVOT if:**
- Interest but wrong audience
- Price objections (test different price)
- Feature gaps revealed

**KILL if:**
- <100 visitors after spending $50+
- Zero payment attempts
- <1% email signup rate
- Feedback is lukewarm

## Pre-Sale Models

### Model 1: Refundable Pre-Order (Recommended)
- Price: 30-50% below launch price
- "Pre-order now, full refund if we don't launch"
- Strongest validation signal

### Model 2: Founding Member
- Higher price ($99-299)
- Lifetime access or deep discount
- For committed early adopters

### Model 3: Paid Waitlist
- $5-29 deposit
- Applied to purchase at launch
- Lower commitment, still real money

## Phase 2 Scoring

After smoke test, calculate Phase 2 score:

| Factor | Points | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Pre-orders received | 0-10 | 3+ = 10 pts |
| Email conversion rate | 0-5 | 5%+ = 5 pts |
| Payment attempts | 0-5 | Any = 3 pts, 5%+ = 5 pts |
| Quality of feedback | 0-5 | Detailed objections = valuable |
| Traffic efficiency | 0-5 | <$1/visitor = 5 pts |

**Phase 2 Score: /30**

**Combined Score = Phase 1 (/30) + Phase 2 (/30) = /60**

**Thresholds:**
- 40+ /60: Strong candidate for building
- 30-39 /60: Consider with caution
- <30 /60: Kill or significant pivot needed

# Universal Scoring Rubric

All product opportunities (digital products, SaaS, subscriptions) use this same rubric for apples-to-apples comparison.

## Scoring Scale

Each criterion: **1 (Poor) → 5 (Excellent)**
Total possible: **30 points**

---

## Criterion 1: Demand (1-5)

*Is there proof people want this?*

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|------------|
| 1 | No evidence; just an idea |
| 2 | Some forum posts asking for it; no products exist |
| 3 | A few competitors with modest sales/traction |
| 4 | Multiple competitors with proven sales; search volume 1k+/mo |
| 5 | Hot market; competitors making $10k+/mo; high search volume; active communities |

**Evidence types:** Competitor sales data, search volume, Reddit/forum activity, waitlist signups, direct customer requests.

---

## Criterion 2: Competition (1-5)

*Can we realistically compete and win?*

| Score | Indicators |
|-------|------------|
| 1 | Dominated by 1-2 massive players; no room |
| 2 | Very crowded; hard to differentiate |
| 3 | Moderate competition; some clear gaps |
| 4 | Few competitors; obvious weaknesses to exploit |
| 5 | Underserved niche; competitors weak or nonexistent (but demand proven) |

**Note:** Zero competitors is usually a 2, not a 5 — it often means no market.

---

## Criterion 3: Revenue Potential (1-5)

*What's the realistic income ceiling?*

| Score | Digital Products | Subscriptions |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 1 | <$200/mo realistic | <$500 MRR realistic |
| 2 | $200-500/mo | $500-1k MRR |
| 3 | $500-1k/mo | $1-3k MRR |
| 4 | $1-3k/mo | $3-10k MRR |
| 5 | >$3k/mo | >$10k MRR |

**Based on:** Competitor revenue (visible or estimated), market size, price point × realistic volume.

---

## Criterion 4: Build Effort (1-5)

*How hard is it to ship v1?*

| Score | Effort |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | >1 month; complex integrations; specialized skills needed |
| 2 | 2-4 weeks; moderate complexity |
| 3 | 1-2 weeks; straightforward build |
| 4 | A few days; mostly assembly/customization |
| 5 | <1 day; template/existing tools; minimal dev |

**Consider:** Dev time, design needs, integrations, testing, content creation.

---

## Criterion 5: Audience Quality (1-5)

*Do the buyers have money and willingness to pay?*

| Score | Audience |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Hobbyists, students, price-sensitive consumers |
| 2 | Mixed; some paying customers but lots of tire-kickers |
| 3 | Prosumers; willing to pay for quality |
| 4 | Professionals; tools are business expenses |
| 5 | Businesses (B2B); budget exists; clear ROI story |

**Signals:** Price points competitors charge, review sentiment around pricing, industry norms.

---

## Criterion 6: Differentiation (1-5)

*Do we have a clear angle to win?*

| Score | Differentiation |
|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | Me-too product; no clear advantage |
| 2 | Minor improvements; easy to copy |
| 3 | Solid angle; some defensibility |
| 4 | Clear gap in market we can own |
| 5 | Unique positioning; strong moat potential |

**Valid angles:** Quality, comprehensiveness, simplicity, price (not race to bottom), niche focus, modern UX, better support.

---

## Thresholds

| Total Score | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| **24-30** | Strong candidate → Prepare full proposal for Alan |
| **18-23** | Promising but weak spots → Identify improvements or pivot angle |
| **<18** | Pass → Log in NICHES-EXPLORED.md with reasoning |

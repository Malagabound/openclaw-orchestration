# Rex SaaS Opportunity Scoring Criteria

Crystal clear standards for evaluating software subscription opportunities.

## 🚨 REGULATED/COMPLIANCE INDUSTRIES - AUTO-REJECT (IRONCLAD)

**IMMEDIATE RED FLAG KEYWORDS - DO NOT RESEARCH:**
- **regulated, compliance, regulatory, government, EPA, FDA, OSHA, HIPAA, SOX, PCI**
- **medical, healthcare, pharmaceutical, finance, banking, legal, insurance**
- **trucking regulations, medical waste, lab animal care, environmental compliance**
- **prompt engineering, AI prompts, prompt chains** (saturated 2022-era market)

**IF ANY OF THESE APPEAR → STOP IMMEDIATELY. DO NOT SCORE. MOVE ON.**

## 1. DEMAND (1-5)

**5 - Exceptional:** 
- Search volume >10k/month for core keywords
- Active communities discussing need (Reddit, Discord, forums)
- Existing SaaS solutions have >$1M ARR
- Users actively requesting features/improvements

**4 - Strong:**
- Search volume 3k-10k/month
- Multiple forum posts/complaints about current solutions  
- Existing players raising venture capital
- Clear user pain points documented

**3 - Moderate:**
- Search volume 1k-3k/month
- Some discussion of need online
- Basic existing solutions present
- Problem exists but not urgent

**2 - Weak:**
- Search volume 500-1k/month
- Limited online discussion
- Few or poor existing solutions
- Problem exists but workarounds available

**1 - None:**
- Search volume <500/month
- No online discussion of need
- No existing solutions (red flag)
- Problem doesn't seem real

## 2. COMPETITION (1-5)

**5 - Blue Ocean:**
- No direct competitors
- Adjacent solutions are complex/expensive
- Clear market gap identified
- First-mover advantage possible

**4 - Light Competition:**
- 1-2 direct competitors
- Existing solutions have obvious flaws
- Competitors are poorly positioned
- Differentiation opportunities clear

**3 - Moderate Competition:**
- 3-5 competitors
- Mix of strengths/weaknesses in existing solutions
- Market large enough for multiple players
- Differentiation possible but requires work

**2 - Heavy Competition:**
- 5-10 competitors
- Some well-funded/established players
- Market consolidating
- Differentiation difficult

**1 - Saturated:**
- 10+ competitors
- Dominant market leaders present
- Low-margin commodity space
- No clear differentiation possible

## 3. MONETIZATION (1-5)

**5 - Excellent ($100k+ ARR potential):**
- Enterprise/B2B market
- High willingness to pay ($100+ monthly)
- Clear ROI value proposition
- Upsell/expansion opportunities

**4 - Strong ($50-100k ARR potential):**
- Professional/SMB market
- Moderate willingness to pay ($50-100 monthly)
- Good value proposition
- Some expansion potential

**3 - Moderate ($25-50k ARR potential):**
- Small business market
- Decent willingness to pay ($25-50 monthly)
- Adequate value proposition
- Limited expansion

**2 - Weak ($10-25k ARR potential):**
- Solopreneur/consumer market
- Low willingness to pay ($10-25 monthly)
- Value proposition unclear
- No expansion opportunities

**1 - Poor (<$10k ARR potential):**
- Consumer/hobby market
- Very low willingness to pay (<$10 monthly)
- Weak value proposition
- Race to the bottom pricing

## 4. BUILDABILITY (1-5)

**5 - Very Easy (1-2 weeks):**
- Simple CRUD app
- Standard web technologies
- No complex integrations
- Minimal custom logic

**4 - Easy (3-4 weeks):**
- Moderate complexity
- Some API integrations
- Basic automation features
- Standard architecture patterns

**3 - Moderate (1-2 months):**
- Multiple integrations required
- Complex business logic
- Real-time features needed
- Moderate technical challenges

**2 - Hard (3-4 months):**
- Complex integrations
- Advanced technical requirements
- Significant custom development
- Multiple technical unknowns

**1 - Very Hard (6+ months):**
- Deep technical expertise required
- Cutting-edge technology needed
- Major architectural challenges
- High failure risk

## 5. SCALABILITY (1-5)

**5 - Fully Automated:**
- No manual customer onboarding
- Self-service signup and usage
- Minimal support requirements
- Linear cost scaling

**4 - Mostly Automated:**
- Basic onboarding automation
- Some manual support needed
- Predictable scaling costs
- Standard SaaS metrics

**3 - Semi-Automated:**
- Mixed manual/automated processes
- Regular support requirements
- Moderate scaling challenges
- Some operational complexity

**2 - Labor Intensive:**
- Significant manual processes
- High support requirements
- Scaling requires hiring
- Operational bottlenecks

**1 - Not Scalable:**
- Entirely manual operations
- Constant customer hand-holding
- Linear cost increases with customers
- Unsustainable unit economics

## 6. PASSION FIT (1-5)

**5 - Perfect Fit:**
- Directly uses Alan's real estate knowledge
- Leverages crypto/blockchain expertise
- Combines multiple areas of expertise
- Personal interest in the space

**4 - Strong Fit:**
- Tangentially related to Alan's background
- Uses software development skills heavily
- Moderate personal interest
- Some domain knowledge transferable

**3 - Moderate Fit:**
- Generic software development
- No special domain knowledge required
- Neutral personal interest
- Could learn required knowledge quickly

**2 - Weak Fit:**
- Outside Alan's expertise areas
- Requires significant learning
- Limited personal interest
- May struggle with domain knowledge

**1 - Poor Fit:**
- Completely outside expertise
- No personal interest
- Would require extensive learning
- High probability of giving up

## FINAL SCORING

**Target Score: ≥24/30 for automatic Phase 2**
**Minimum: 20/30 to proceed**

Each criterion scored 1-5, total /30.
Must justify each score with specific evidence.
